Introducing Te Whare Tapa Whā

Te Whare Tapa Whā was developed by leading Māori health advocate Sir Mason Durie in 1984. The model describes health and wellbeing as a wharenui/meeting house with four walls.

These walls represent taha wairua/spiritual wellbeing, taha hinengaro/mental and emotional wellbeing, taha tinana/physical wellbeing and taha whānau/family and social wellbeing. Our connection with the whenua/land forms the foundation.

When all these things are in balance, we thrive. When one or more of these is out of balance our wellbeing is impacted.

Taha Tinana (physical wellbeing)

Healthy eating is am important factor of physical wellbeing, Luckily we do communal cooking in my flat so four nights of the week I have a healthy dinner I don't need to consider. In regards to physical activity I've signed up for a boxing gym around the corner from the Dev Academy campus so I can attend classes before/after study.

Taha Hinengaro (emotional and mental wellbeing)

Mental and emotional wellbeing has always been a struggle for me but I've learned to balance that through healthy communication and journaling. Since it's been introduced at Dev Academy I've found that meditation has really helped to keep me level also.

Taha Wairua (spiritual wellbeing)

I've never been a deeply spiritual person despite finding theology to be a very interesting field of study. I tend to guide my spirtiual wellbeing by understanding my place in the lives of those around me, simply treating people the way I'd like to be treated and trying to be a good person everyday.

Taha Whānau (family wellbeing)

I'm in touch with all of my siblings constantly, having 6 of them means there is always someone to speak with. I have a great relationship with my friends and and flatmates too, not to mention my parter. I'm lucky to see people I love everyday and be able to speak honestly to them also.

Whenua (land)

I'm lucky to live in a beautiful house with a lovely garden, whenever I find myself stuck I go to sit in one of the nooks outside with my cat to unwind and think over things away from my screen, we have veggies and plants that need to be tended to also. The beach is also a 10 minute walk away so I'm very connected to the lan and understand the benefits of immersing myself in it.
